About the Local Bitcoiners Podcast

Local Bitcoiners is a Bitcoin podcast dedicated to Bitcoin meetup culture. Hosts Reed and Rev Hodl sit down with Bitcoin meetup organizers from across the country to learn how they find, build, and grow local Bitcoin communities. Every episode explores what makes a local Bitcoin meetup thrive — from getting your first five attendees to building a lasting culture of grassroots Bitcoin adoption.

Whether you already organize a Bitcoin meetup, attend one, or are curious about starting one in your area, the Local Bitcoiners podcast connects you with the people making it happen on the ground. This isn't about price charts or trading — it's about the real-world communities that form when Bitcoiners meet face to face.

Do you run a Bitcoin meetup and want to talk about it with us? Want more information about Grassroots Bitcoin or the quarterly meetup organizer calls? Reach out: localbitcoiners@gmail.com

Bitcoin Meetup Resources

Local Bitcoiners maintains an open-source resource for anyone looking to start, run, or grow a Bitcoin meetup. It's a community-driven repository covering everything from how to find a venue and get your first attendees to how to structure recurring events and build lasting local Bitcoin culture.

The Bitcoin meetup organizers featured on the Local Bitcoiners podcast consistently share practical advice that we've compiled into this resource. Topics include choosing a format for your Bitcoin meetup (casual social, presentation-based, workshop-style), handling common challenges like venue costs and attendance dips, and ideas for making your local Bitcoin community welcoming to newcomers.

Whether you're spinning up your first meetup or looking for ideas from organizers across the country, this is the place to find and share what works. Contributions welcome — if you've learned something running your Bitcoin meetup, add it.
